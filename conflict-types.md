### **Type I — Direct War & Occupation**

**Definition:**
U.S. forces directly conduct large-scale combat operations (ground troops, sustained bombing, occupation).

**Includes:**

* Invasions
* Declared or undeclared wars
* Long-term occupations

**Examples:**

* Korea
* Vietnam
* Afghanistan (2001–2021)
* Iraq (2003–2011)
* Panama
* Grenada

**Civilian deaths:**
Mostly **direct U.S. killings**, plus secondary effects.

---

### **Type II — Direct Military Intervention (Limited)**

**Definition:**
Short-term or geographically limited U.S. military force **without full-scale war or occupation**.

**Includes:**

* Airstrikes
* Missile strikes
* Raids
* No sustained territorial control

**Examples:**

* Libya (1986 bombing)
* Sudan (1998 Al-Shifa strike)
* Syria (missile strikes)
* Pakistan (drone war)
* Somalia (raids, airstrikes)

**Civilian deaths:**
Primarily **direct U.S. killings**, smaller scale but repeated.

---

### **Type III — Proxy War & Armed Support**

**Definition:**
U.S. funds, arms, trains, or provides intelligence to **local or regional forces** fighting on its behalf.

**Includes:**

* CIA covert operations
* Arming militias or insurgents
* Military aid during active wars

**Examples:**

* Afghanistan (Mujahideen, 1979–1989)
* El Salvador
* Nicaragua (Contras)
* Yemen (Saudi-led war support)
* Syria (armed opposition support)
* Ukraine

**Civilian deaths:**
Mostly **U.S.-enabled deaths**, often **much larger** than direct killings.

---

### **Type IV — Political Destabilization & Regime Change**

**Definition:**
U.S. actively undermines or removes governments **without large-scale direct combat**.

**Includes:**

* Coups
* Election interference
* Economic warfare tied to unrest
* Support for post-coup regimes

**Examples:**

* Iran (1953)
* Guatemala (1954)
* Chile (1973)
* Indonesia (1965)
* Haiti (multiple episodes)
* Venezuela (attempted regime change)

**Civilian deaths:**
Almost entirely **indirect**, often through repression, civil war, or dictatorship.
