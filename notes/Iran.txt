### Direct US–Iran military clashes and operations

- 1980: Operation Eagle Claw – failed US special forces mission in Iran to rescue American hostages in Tehran; aircraft collision kills 8 US personnel in Tabas desert. [irregularwarfare](https://irregularwarfare.org/articles/us-iranian-irregular-warfare-history/)
- 1987–1988: “Tanker War” phase of Iran–Iraq War – US Navy protects Gulf shipping; numerous skirmishes with Iranian forces, mining incidents, and exchanges of fire. [aljazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/6/23/history-of-us-iran-relations-from-the-1953-regime-change-to-trump-strikes)
- April 18, 1988: Operation Praying Mantis – large US naval attack on Iranian warships and oil platforms in retaliation for mining USS Samuel B. Roberts; Iran loses several vessels and platforms. [en.wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iran%E2%80%93United_States_relations)
- July 3, 1988: USS Vincennes shoots down Iran Air Flight 655 over the Gulf while engaged with Iranian naval units, killing 290 civilians; incident occurs in context of US–Iran naval clashes. [aljazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/6/23/history-of-us-iran-relations-from-the-1953-regime-change-to-trump-strikes)
- 2003–2011 and after: Iraq – US forces repeatedly clash with Iran‑backed Shia militias (e.g., Kataib Hezbollah, Asaib Ahl al‑Haq) using Iranian‑supplied IEDs, rockets, and EFPs; hundreds of US casualties attributed to Iranian support. [amu.apus](https://www.amu.apus.edu/area-of-study/legal-studies/resources/us-iran-relations/)
- 2011–present: Syria and Iraq – periodic US airstrikes on Iranian‑backed militias (IRGC‑linked groups, Kataib Hezbollah, etc.) after rocket/drone attacks on US bases. [cfr](https://www.cfr.org/global-conflict-tracker/conflict/confrontation-between-united-states-and-iran)
- January 3, 2020: US drone strike near Baghdad Airport kills IRGC‑Quds Force commander Qassem Soleimani and Iraqi militia leader Abu Mahdi al‑Muhandis. [pbs](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/a-timeline-of-u-s-iran-relations)
- January 8, 2020: Iran launches ballistic missiles at US‑used bases in Iraq (Ain al‑Asad and Erbil) in retaliation; over 100 US personnel suffer traumatic brain injuries. [pbs](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/a-timeline-of-u-s-iran-relations)
- October 26, 2023: US airstrikes on Iran‑linked facilities in Syria following attacks on US forces. [cfr](https://www.cfr.org/global-conflict-tracker/conflict/confrontation-between-united-states-and-iran)
- February 2, 2024: US conducts large wave of strikes on more than 80 Iran‑affiliated targets in Iraq and Syria after deadly militia drone attack on US troops in Jordan. [cfr](https://www.cfr.org/global-conflict-tracker/conflict/confrontation-between-united-states-and-iran)
- June 22, 2025: Operation “Midnight Hammer” – US Air Force and Navy strike three Iranian nuclear facilities (Fordow, Natanz, Isfahan) with bunker‑buster bombs and cruise missiles during Iran–Israel war; Iran fires missiles at US base in Qatar in response. [en.wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_strikes_on_Iranian_nuclear_sites)

### Iranian and Iran‑backed attacks on US targets

- 1983: Beirut barracks bombing – truck bombing by group linked to Hezbollah, backed by Iran, kills 241 US servicemembers in Lebanon. [irregularwarfare](https://irregularwarfare.org/articles/us-iranian-irregular-warfare-history/)
- 1980s–1990s: Multiple kidnappings and attacks on US personnel in Lebanon by Iranian‑backed groups (including Hezbollah); US attributes responsibility to Iran. [irregularwarfare](https://irregularwarfare.org/articles/us-iranian-irregular-warfare-history/)
- 1996: Khobar Towers bombing in Saudi Arabia – attack on US Air Force housing kills 19 Americans; US officials later blame Iran‑backed Saudi Hezbollah. [amu.apus](https://www.amu.apus.edu/area-of-study/legal-studies/resources/us-iran-relations/)
- 2003–2011: Iraq – sustained campaign of IED, EFP, rocket, and mortar attacks by Iran‑backed militias against US forces. [amu.apus](https://www.amu.apus.edu/area-of-study/legal-studies/resources/us-iran-relations/)
- 2011–present: Iraq/Syria – recurring rockets, mortars, and drones on US bases by Iran‑aligned militias; hundreds of attacks recorded, especially 2019–2025. [fdd](https://www.fdd.org/analysis/2025/06/19/iranian-and-iranian-backed-attacks-against-americans-1979-present/)
- February 2021: Rocket from Iran‑backed militia hits coalition base in Erbil, wounding US personnel and contractors. [fdd](https://www.fdd.org/analysis/2025/06/19/iranian-and-iranian-backed-attacks-against-americans-1979-present/)
- July 2021: Series of rocket and drone attacks by Iran‑backed militias wound US service members in Iraq and Syria. [fdd](https://www.fdd.org/analysis/2025/06/19/iranian-and-iranian-backed-attacks-against-americans-1979-present/)
- September 2022: Iranian rocket attack in Iraqi Kurdistan kills an American citizen. [fdd](https://www.fdd.org/analysis/2025/06/19/iranian-and-iranian-backed-attacks-against-americans-1979-present/)
- March 2023: Iran‑made drone kills a US contractor and wounds US troops at a base near Hasakah, Syria. [fdd](https://www.fdd.org/analysis/2025/06/19/iranian-and-iranian-backed-attacks-against-americans-1979-present/)
- October 17, 2023 – November 19, 2024: Over 180 attacks by Iran and proxies on US forces in Iraq, Syria, and region, causing multiple US casualties. [cfr](https://www.cfr.org/global-conflict-tracker/conflict/confrontation-between-united-states-and-iran)

### Proxy and indirect conflicts involving both

- 1980–1988: Iran–Iraq War – US provides extensive political, intelligence, and some military support to Iraq, including naval protection and clashes with Iran during the Tanker War. [aljazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/6/23/history-of-us-iran-relations-from-the-1953-regime-change-to-trump-strikes)
- 1982–present: Lebanon – Iran builds and backs Hezbollah; Hezbollah engages US and US‑aligned targets (Beirut bombings, hostage‑taking, later rocket threats), creating long‑term indirect confrontation. [irregularwarfare](https://irregularwarfare.org/articles/us-iranian-irregular-warfare-history/)
- 2003–present: Iraq – Iran‑backed Shia militias fight US presence and later US‑led coalition; US and Iran back opposing Iraqi factions at various stages. [amu.apus](https://www.amu.apus.edu/area-of-study/legal-studies/resources/us-iran-relations/)
- 2011–present: Syria – US backs some opposition and fights ISIS; Iran (through IRGC and Hezbollah) backs Assad; both operate militarily in same theater on opposing sides and occasionally exchange limited fire via proxies. [cfr](https://www.cfr.org/global-conflict-tracker/conflict/confrontation-between-united-states-and-iran)
- 2015–present: Yemen – Iran supports Houthi movement; US backs Saudi‑led coalition and conducts limited strikes; Houthis attack US and partner shipping in Red Sea and Gulf of Aden, bringing indirect US–Iran confrontation. [irregularwarfare](https://irregularwarfare.org/articles/us-iranian-irregular-warfare-history/)
- 2014–present: Cyber domain – repeated US and Iran cyber operations and intrusions against each other’s government and infrastructure targets. [crisisgroup](https://www.crisisgroup.org/trigger-list/iran-us-trigger-list)

### Nuclear, maritime, and “single‑hit” confrontations
- 2007–2019: Numerous incidents in the Gulf (boat swarms, warning shots, aircraft intercepts) between US Navy and IRGC Navy in Strait of Hormuz and nearby waters. [crisisgroup](https://www.crisisgroup.org/trigger-list/iran-us-trigger-list)
- 2010s: Covert sabotage operations and cyberattacks on Iran’s nuclear program (e.g., Stuxnet), widely reported as involving US and Israel. [basicint](https://basicint.org/us-and-israeli-strikes-on-iran-a-timeline-of-irans-nuclear-programme/)
- 2019: Series of tanker attacks near Gulf of Oman and drone shoot‑downs; US blames Iran and launches limited retaliatory cyber and planned (partly canceled) kinetic responses. [en.wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iran%E2%80%93United_States_relations)
- April 2024–2025: Iran‑backed Houthis and other proxies attack commercial and sometimes US‑associated vessels; US responds with naval interceptions and airstrikes on launch sites. [crisisgroup](https://www.crisisgroup.org/trigger-list/iran-us-trigger-list)
- June 22, 2025: Single‑night US strikes on Fordow, Natanz, Isfahan nuclear sites inside Iran (Operation Midnight Hammer), the first acknowledged large‑scale US bombing of mainland Iran. [en.wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_strikes_on_Iranian_nuclear_sites)
